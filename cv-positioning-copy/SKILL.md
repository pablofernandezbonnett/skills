# CV Positioning Copy

## Overview

Use this skill when working on a CV, LinkedIn profile, cover letter, or candidate summary that must stay aligned with a source-of-truth profile while still sounding strong enough to win interviews.

Default goal:
- preserve credibility
- improve clarity and punch
- avoid buzzword-heavy or AI-optimized wording
- keep all outputs aligned with the candidate profile

## Workflow

1. Start from the source of truth.
   In this repo, that is usually `JobHunting/CANDIDATE_PROFILE.yaml`.

2. Confirm what kind of artifact you are editing.
   Common cases:
   - `master CV`
   - offer-specific CV
   - LinkedIn profile
   - cover letter

3. Keep the same truth, change only the framing.
   Do not invent a new narrative per document. Reweight emphasis based on the artifact and role.

4. Prefer proof over wording.
   Replace abstract claims with concrete systems, flows, stakeholders, integrations, or responsibilities.

5. Keep platform, project, and exposure levels precise.
   Example: distinguish a platform such as `SAP Commerce / Hybris` from a project such as `SAP Store`.

6. Keep situational seniority visible when it is real.
   If the candidate was trusted to mentor, coordinate day-to-day work, unblock others, or act as the practical bridge with stakeholders, preserve that signal without upgrading it into a formal leadership title.

7. Preserve backend-first identity without hiding adjacent contribution.
   If the candidate is fundamentally backend-oriented but has contributed in React, TypeScript, Flutter, or Dart when the product needed it, keep that visible in a way that supports flexibility without diluting core positioning.

8. Do not imply local market or customer context from physical location alone.
   Being based in Japan does not automatically mean working for Japanese clients, using Japanese internally, or having Japan-market product exposure unless that is explicitly true.

9. If the wording starts to sound generic, loaded, or too optimized, simplify it.
   The target tone is direct, human, and interview-defendable.

## Document Rules

### Master CV

- Optimize for fast recruiter understanding.
- Keep the identity stable.
- Preserve the strongest evidence near the top.
- Compress older roles before weakening strong recent roles.
- If space is tight, remove redundancy before removing signals.
- Keep informal leadership signals when they were real and repeated, but phrase them as situational trust or senior support rather than formal management.
- Keep backend identity primary even when adjacent frontend or mobile work appears.

### LinkedIn

- Keep it broader than the CV, but not less truthful.
- Let the `About` read naturally.
- Keep backend/product identity central even when adjacent frontend or mobile work appears.

### Cover Letters

- Start from role fit, not autobiography.
- Use only experience that clearly maps to the company or role pressure.
- Do not claim missing depth just because the job description asks for it.

## Source-of-Truth Order

When there is tension, prefer this order:
1. candidate profile
2. verified work history and real examples
3. market positioning
4. job-description keyword coverage
